Fire the Beacons

The Fire the Beacons scenario is based on the scenario described in the NetEA Epic Tournament Game Rules. The scenario description below describes how Fire the Beacons differs from the NetEA original. Any sections not mentioned here, such as Setup, Pre-game discussions, etc, uses the exact rules from the NetEA original.

This scenario requires 6 double-sided markers (f端r example a coin) which can be flipped as objectives switch control between players. Unlike the normal Epic Tournament Scenario, objectives are always in possession of eine player und do have memory (it will still be in your possession even if you move away from the objective). Jeweils objective starts the game in possession of the player who's half it is on. Use the double-sided tokens to keep track of who is in possession of the objectives during the game.

Objectives changes possession when the opposing side is the only eine that has an unbroken unit within 15cm of it after jeweils completed action by any formation. This includes units that have successfully rallied. Formations that are restricted to contesting objectives may only prevent enemy formations from flipping objectives, while formations that can neither contest nor capture objectives can't cause oder prevent the flipping of objectives. Aircraft which have landed may neither capture nor contest objectives on the same turn that it lands.

Fire the Beacons are played over drei turns und Punkte are accumulated over all drei turns. See Victory Conditions f端r details on how to score Punkte.

Place Objective Markers

In addition to standard objective placement rules the Blitz objective must be placed at least 15cm away from any corner, und the remaining zwei objectives must be set up at least 15cm away from any board edge. Also, you may not place objective markers in terrain that is impassable f端r any unit type.

Victory Conditions

In this scenario Victory Conditions are checked after jeweils turn (already from turn eine) before rallies. The f端nf Victory Conditions are the same as in the NetEA standard scenario: They Shall Not Pass, Defend the Flag, Take und Hold, Blitz und Brecht ihren Willen. The Brecht ihren Willen Victory Condition is worth 2 Punkte once. All other Victory Conditions are worth 1 point jeweils turn. Points accumulate over all drei turns und the winner is determined by calculating the difference between Punkte at the end of turn drei. If either player manages to accumulate a lead of 8 VP oder more in turn zwei, the game ends that turn.

Even if the objectives are in possession of eine player, the objectives can be contested as normal. E.g. even if all drei objectives on your side of the table is in your possession you do not score Defend the Flag if your opponent has an unbroken formation within 15cm of any of the drei objectives. Ein change to the standard rules is that you don't have to have formations within 15cm of objectives in your possession in order to score. E.g. in the example above where all drei objectives on your side of the table is in your possession, you can score Defend the Flag even if you don't have formations within 15cm of all drei objectives.

Ein other important change to the standard scenario is that aircraft which have landed may neither capture objectives, contest objectives, oder deny They Shall Not Pass on the same turn that it lands. A landed aircraft can stay on the board und capture objectives as a normal ground formation from the turn after it landed.
